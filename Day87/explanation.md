## Day 87 – SQL for Analytics (Conceptual Explanation)

KPIs (Key Performance Indicators)
are measurable values
used to evaluate business performance.

SQL is widely used
to calculate KPIs from data.

---

### Common KPIs

Important business metrics include:

- Total Revenue
- Average Order Value (AOV)
- Number of Orders
- Customer Revenue
- Daily/Monthly Trends

---

### Revenue Metrics

Revenue metrics help understand:

- business growth
- performance over time
- customer value

---

### Aggregation in Analytics

Aggregation functions are heavily used:

- SUM() → total revenue
- AVG() → average value
- COUNT() → total records

---

### Group-Based Analysis

GROUP BY helps analyze:

- customer-level metrics
- date-wise trends
- region-based insights

---

### Time-Based Analysis

Tracking metrics over time helps:

- identify trends
- detect seasonality
- monitor growth

---

### Real-World Applications

KPIs are used in:

- dashboards
- business reports
- marketing analytics
- product analysis

---

### Interview Importance

Common interview questions:

- Calculate revenue metrics
- Find top customers
- Analyze trends
- Compute averages

These are core data analyst tasks.

---

### Summary

SQL for analytics focuses on:

- KPIs and metrics
- aggregation functions
- grouping and trends
- business insights

This is essential for data roles.
